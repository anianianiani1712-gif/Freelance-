The 4-Part Pitch Plan

🎣
The Hook ~15 seconds

Open with a question, a surprising fact, or an "imagine if…" moment. Make the sharks lean in before you explain anything. Don't introduce the business yet — just create curiosity.

Have you ever noticed that…? / Imagine a world where… / Did you know that 1 in 3 people…?
🔥
The Problem ~20 seconds

Name the pain point clearly and make the sharks feel it. Say who has this problem, how often it happens, and why it matters. Don't just describe it — make it real and relatable.

The problem is that many people… / Currently, there is no easy way to… / Millions of people still have to…
💡
The Solution ~30 seconds

Introduce your product or service. One clear sentence on what it does, then one sentence on why it's better than what already exists. If you need more than two sentences, it's too complicated.

That's why we created… / Our product allows you to… / Unlike our competitors, we…
📊
The Business ~30 seconds

Cover three things quickly: who your customers are, how you make money, and one number that shows market potential. Be specific and confident — investors need to see a path to profit.

Our target market is… / We make money by… / There are X million potential customers, which means…
👁
Eye contact with all the sharks, not just one person
🐢
Slow down for numbers — sharks need a moment to process them
📄
Don't read from notes — glance down, then look up and speak
🤷
If stuck: "That's a great point — our plan is to…"

Language support

Useful Expressions

Pitching your idea
  • Have you ever noticed that…?
  • The problem we're solving is…
  • That's why we created / developed…
  • Unlike our competitors, we…
  • Our target market is… because…
  • We make money by… / We charge…
  • We expect to break even within…
  • What makes us unique is…
Sharks — asking questions
  • What is your USP?
  • Who are your main competitors?
  • How will you scale?
  • What's your biggest risk?
  • Could you explain that in more detail?
  • How soon will you break even?
  • What stops a bigger company from copying this?
Sharks — reacting
  • I'm definitely in for…
  • I'm out — the risk is too high.
  • I'd invest, but only if you lower…
  • I'm not convinced by the numbers.
  • I can see real potential here.
  • I need more evidence before committing.
  • That's a lot to ask. Could you justify…?

Answering difficult questions

If you don't know → "That's a great point. Our current plan is to…"
If you disagree → "I understand your concern, but I think…"
If you need time → "Could I come back to that? First, I'd like to mention…"
